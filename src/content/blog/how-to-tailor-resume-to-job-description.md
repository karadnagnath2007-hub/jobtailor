Sending the same resume to every job is like wearing a suit to the beach — technically fine, but completely missing the point. Tailoring your resume to each job description is the single most effective thing you can do to increase your interview rate.

## Why Tailoring Matters So Much

Recruiters spend an average of 7.4 seconds scanning a resume. In that time, they're looking for signals that you match what they asked for. A tailored resume makes those signals impossible to miss.

Beyond human readers, ATS software ranks candidates by keyword match. A generic resume might match 40% of keywords. A tailored one can hit 80-90%.

## The 4-Step Tailoring Process

### Step 1: Read the Job Description Like a Detective

Highlight the must-have requirements. Look for:
- Required skills and technologies
- Years of experience
- Industry-specific terminology
- Soft skills mentioned repeatedly
- Action verbs used ("lead," "drive," "execute")

### Step 2: Match Your Experience to Their Language

This is the critical step. Take your accomplishments and rephrase them using the employer's language.

**Example:**
- Job says: "Manage cross-functional teams to deliver product features"
- You wrote: "Worked with designers and engineers to build new things"
- Tailored: "Led cross-functional team of 8 designers and engineers to deliver 12 product features"

### Step 3: Reorder Your Bullet Points

Put the most relevant accomplishments first. If the job emphasizes "data analysis," your data-related bullet goes at the top of that role's description.

### Step 4: Update Your Summary or Headline

If you have a professional summary, rewrite it to echo the job's priorities. One to two sentences that position you as the exact solution to their problem.
